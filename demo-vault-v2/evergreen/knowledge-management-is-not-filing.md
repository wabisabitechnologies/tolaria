# Knowledge Management Is Not Filing
The dominant metaphor for personal knowledge management is the filing cabinet: put things in labeled folders, retrieve when needed. This metaphor is mostly wrong.

Knowledge becomes useful through connection, not storage. An idea you've filed carefully but never connected to your other ideas is almost as useless as an idea you forgot.

The goal of a note-taking system should be to maximize the probability of unexpected connections — serendipitous collisions between ideas from different domains. This is why spatial proximity (notes near related notes) and links matter more than tags or folders.
